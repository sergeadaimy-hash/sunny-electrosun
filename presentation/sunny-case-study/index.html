Two Claude models, used for what each does best.

Claude Sonnet 4.6

Per call
~$0.005

Why split? Reply quality stays at Opus level. Background calls run on Sonnet. Result: $20 a day covers around 600 customer messages without compromise.

13 Memory

Sunny doesn't ask the same question twice.

Before every reply, Sunny builds a structured state object: what the customer has said, what's been asked, what's pending.

This becomes a system block in the prompt, alongside the warehouse and the master rules.

Result: no "and how big is your system?" five times. The conversation moves forward every turn.

Conversation state, current turn

    kw: 16,
    phase: "3-phase",

    "installer_or_end_user",

30 Human in the loop

The owner can jump into any conversation. Sunny stops typing.

1

Take over

Owner clicks "Take over" in inbox. Sunny pauses on this conversation. The contact does not see anything change.

2

Type manually

Owner types replies from admin. The customer sees them as if from Sunny. Pastel violet bubbles in admin mark human-typed messages.

3

Release or walk away

Owner clicks "Release", or simply walks away. Auto-release threshold (15 min default) returns the conversation to Sunny.

The last unanswered message gets re-queued, no double replies. Sunny picks up exactly where the owner left off.
