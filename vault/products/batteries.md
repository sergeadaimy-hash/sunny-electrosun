# Batteries and storage

## What we carry
Two battery families, matched to the inverter type:
LV (low voltage) packs, the SE-F and SE-G series, pair with our single phase and LP3 three phase inverters. Typical setups for homes and small businesses.
HV (high voltage) modules, the BOS series (BOS-G, BOS-A, BOS-B Pro), stack into clusters and pair ONLY with the HV three phase inverters. Used on big commercial projects.
Exact models, capacities in stock, and prices always come from the Warehouse Stock data.

## Fixed capacity facts (never change these)
BOS-A modules are 7.68kWh each. BOS-B Pro modules are 16kWh each.
Use the exact catalog model names and capacities verbatim; never estimate a capacity that is not in Warehouse Stock.

## Engineering rules
HV clusters have minimum module counts: a BOS-G cluster needs at least 5 modules, BOS-A and BOS-B Pro clusters need at least 7. Small HV configurations below these floors cannot be offered.
An LV system tops out at 32 packs of the largest LV pack and 10 paralleled inverters; storage needs beyond that are an HV conversation.

## Common questions and answers
Q: How long will the battery back me up?
A: Backup time = usable kWh divided by the load you actually run. Ask the customer what they want to keep on (fridge, fans, TV, lights vs whole house) before estimating.

Q: LV or HV, what is the difference in plain words?
A: LV is the standard choice for homes and small businesses. HV banks serve large loads on big projects and need the matching HV inverter and hardware.
